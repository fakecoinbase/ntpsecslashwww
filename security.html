Security Researchers and Vulnerability Reports

Invitation to Responsible Security Researchers

We specifically invite and welcome the scrutiny and participation of responsible security researchers. Please, inspect the code, abuse the APIs, fuzz the sockets, and attack the network traffic.

Reporting Vulnerabilities

Please report discovered vulnerabiltites to mailto:security@ntpsec.org.

This email alias forwards to a very small cadre of the internal NTPsec team.

Our goal is to ack receipt within 24 hours, verify each potential vulnerability within 3 days, and if the vulnerability is a significant network risk, such as remote execution, denial of service, network amplification, or corruption of time reporting, develop a fix within 7 days.

Our experience so far is that we are much faster than that.

Reporter Responsible Disclosure Policies

We honor responsible disclosure policies and embargos requested by responsible security researchers and security research organizations.

Vulnerabilities properly reported to us are tracked in a controlled access issue tracker. Development of fixes is done in private Git repos. Issues and fixes will opened to the public when the reasonable embargo expires.

If we receive vulnerability reports that are duplicates, we will put the reporters in contact with each other, and then will honor the shortest embargo.

Our Responsible Disclosure Policies

As NTPsec is currently in developer pre-release and is not yet deployed in production, we current have no responsible disclosure or embargo policies of our own. As we find issues and vulnerabilities ourselves and by regular contributors, we will verify and fix on our existing development and release cadence, in our public issue tracker and on our public Git repositories.

Once NTPsec is in production use on the public internet or by significant users in their own datacenters, we will work towards an industry standard best practices Responsible Disclosure and Embargo Policy.
